Support Us

HMS is proud of its achievements. We pay our own basic running and rehearsal costs, but are dependent on our supporters to enable us to realise our musical aspirations, particularly to help us with the increasing cost of staging concerts and hiring top-class soloists and other professionals. Even with a full house, our ticket and programme sales do not cover expenses.

We also seek to encourage students to join us by halving our normal subscription fees, and by promoting participation in concerts by children at local schools. New para Please help us to realise these goals by contributing financially.

  • Promoting participation in concerts by children at local schools. HMS members themselves pay the basic running and rehearsal costs, but depend on supporters to help maintain financial independence - and thus artistic freedom.

Become a Friend

HMS Friends are the vital core of an appreciative and discerning audience, and are a sociable group of like-minded music lovers.

Friends are kept in touch with our plans and activities through regular newsletters from the Secretary, who is a member of the choir.

A Friends subscription is just £20 per annum, and you will receive our newsletter and other relevant communications. We will also include your name in our concert programmes and on our website

Joint membership is available at £35 per annum and provides all the benefits of individual membership for two people sharing a joint subscription and mailings to a single address.

Become a Patron

We are especially grateful for annual donations from our Patrons.

A Patrons subscription is £50 per annum, and in addition to the benefits accruing to our Friends, we further show our appreciation by offering you a complimentary programme and a free interval drink, inviting you to our post-concert parties and other social events, and giving you the opportunity to attend rehearsals. You will also have a Patrons card.

Joint membership is available from £90 per annum for Patrons and provides all the benefits of individual membership for two people sharing a joint subscription and mailings to a single address.

Gift Aid

If you are a UK taxpayer, using Gift Aid means that for every pound you give, we get an extra 25p from the Inland Revenue, helping your donation go further.

If you are a higher rate taxpayer, you can claim back the difference between the basic and higher rate tax paid, reducing the net cost to you of your gift.

Example: A higher-rate taxpayer who becomes a Patron of HMS with a donation of £100 would incur a net cost of just £75 per annum, see below:

Legacies

Why (not) leave a legacy to HMS?

If you have valued your association with HMS, whether as a singing member, orchestral member or as part of the audience, you will know how much the Society has given to you, perhaps over many years, through the magnificence, beauty and emotional power of music.

We need your help to safeguard the future of HMS and to ensure that the next generation of singers, players and audience may experience HMS as you have known and loved it.

If you would like to leave a legacy to HMS in your will, your best course of action is to arrange this through your own solicitor. If you prefer, the Chairman will be pleased to put you in touch with an experienced solicitor, connected to HMS, who can offer you professional assistance.

HMS is very grateful for all legacies and is happy to recognise your generosity, if you wish, with an acknowledgement in our concert programme. If you are able to leave £5000 or more, we will be pleased to dedicate a concert in your memory.
