March 8 Concert: Brief Report

The HMS concert on 8 March 2014 proved a tour de force for both choir and orchestra. It is a curious fact that, for a society over 90 years old, the occasion was almost a coming of age. The orchestra, probably better than it has ever been, gave a moving and intensely rich performance of Dvorak's 6th symphony.

The choir, who had wrestled for two months with a difficult Bruckner Mass and had occasionally wondered whether it would ever work, ended the evening to rapturous applause, feeling that this evening, more than anything in their recent past, had defined them as entirely worthy of the faithful audiences who come out, year after year, to hear them perform.

As a society, choir and orchestra together, they are on a high which should long sustain them as they head to their centenary.
