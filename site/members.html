Highlights of 2012-2013

In October 2012 we had a wonderful Orchestral concert in St Christopher's church with Mozart's overture to Don Giovanni, the Delius Song before Sunrise and Schubert's Symphony No 6. The highlight of the evening was Helen Owlett's beautiful clarinet playing in Weber's Clarinet Concerto and Finzi's Five Bagatelles.

We are indeed fortunate to have such talent in our Orchestra. The December concert was a sell out for The Messiah (excerpts) and Beethoven's Symphony No 6, the Pastoral. Both were enthusiastically received by the large audience. In February we had a hugely successful Come and Sing in St Christopher's Church when Christopher Hann, our new Chorus Master, rehearsed a choir of over 100 in Fauré's Requiem with Clive Osgood on the organ.

We were then joined by a small orchestra (mostly HMS members) for the performance which added much to the pleasure of the event for all present. Christopher has been a great success in everything he has done with us throughout his first season with us.

The performance of Mendelssohn's Elijah in March was outstanding. Orchestra and Chorus were at their best on the night and the soloists were superb. Thanks to the sterling efforts of members of the Society in publicising the concert and selling tickets we had a near full house with only a few unsold seats downstairs.

The 2012 - 2013 HMS season ended on 4th May with a widely acclaimed concert of Opera Highlights. This was a lighter than usual programme of music by Rossini, Donizetti, Gounod, Verdi, Mascagni, Puccini and Wagner which was performed by the orchestra and chorus with great enthusiasm. An innovation was the inclusion of arias sung by three St Edmund's School (Hindhead) choristers.

They delighted the audience with their remarkable vocal skill and their poise on stage. The soprano soloist in Mascagni's Easter Hymn from Cavalleria Rusticana was local singer Joanna Bywater who gave a radiant performance. Altogether the concert was recognised as a tremendous tribute to local talent.

During June the Society traditionally has four sessions which are open to anyone who wishes to play an instrument or sing for pleasure. These Summer Sessions have different conductors who rehearse and then run through different works. In the first half of the first session the orchestra leader, Tom Horn, guided the orchestra through Dukas' Sorcerer's Apprentice, a challenging piece which in the final run through conjured up images of Disney's Mickey Mouse in the film Fantasia.

The orchestra and choir came together after the break and under the baton of Chorus Master Christopher Hann produced a rousing performance of Handel's Zadok the Priest and The King Shall Rejoice.

The second session also involved both orchestra and choir. Tom Horn conducted the orchestra for two pieces, one by Rawsthorne and one by Debussy while Sarah Thomas, a music student, rehearsed Parry's I was Glad with the choir. The final run-through involved orchestra and choir energetically performing the Parry under the baton of Sarah.

There was an innovation in the third session when the orchestra split into separate string and wind sections. Christopher Hann took the strings for Dvořák's delightful Serenade while the wind section enjoyed rehearsing another Dvořák work, his Czech Suite under the baton of guest conductor, Bob Haselip.

For the final session guest conductor Mark Cardy accepted the challenge to conduct two movements from Holst's Planets Suite: Mars and Jupiter. This time the session was held in Grayswood Village Hall, not the Haslemere Hall and the orchestra managed to produce a resounding sound in the final run-through.

The four sessions proved the value of giving the opportunity to musicians to experience a wide range of music and conducting styles and to tackle new works with great enjoyment.

HMS Anniversary Party

What a great way to end the HMS 90th Anniversary year in 2013!

Members enjoyed a glass of mulled wine and tucked in to Lorraine Collins' wonderful Celebration Cake which not only looked great but was also delicious!

December 2013 was a memorable month for HMS members. The concert on the 7th was a great success and much admired by both the audience and the reviewer. A special triumph was the re-incarnation of the Beethoven Eroica Symphony, played with piano support at the first HMS concert in 1923 but this time played with aplomb by the full orchestra. The choir also excelled itself in the Vivaldi Gloria and the Bach Wachet Auf winning accolades all round.

  • No Rehearsal on Easter Monday 21st April
  • Summer Sessions 2014: 2nd & 9th June in Haslemere Hall, 16th and 23rd June in St Christopher's Church

Rehearsals are in Haslemere Hall on Mondays at 7.30pm till about 10pm for both choir and orchestra. We have a tea/coffee break about half way through. There is normally a rehearsal during the afternoon before each concert. Occasionally the choir rehearses in the Methodist church in Weyhill when Haslemere Hall is not available. The orchestra also sometimes rehearses in St Christopher's Church, Weyhill.

Cranleigh Choral Week, 10-17 August
HMS members are invited to be part of the UK's largest single week adult choral course!

This hugely successful summer course is the largest single week gathering of adult choral singers in the country. Its blend of large chorus and chamber choir repertoire, excellent choral tutors and mix of singing and social activities make it a very popular course for singers of all abilities and backgrounds.

Rehearse and perform choral works (with orchestra) and chamber choir music in the same week.

The 2014 South of England Festival Chorus work will be:

Elgar's DREAM OF GERONTIUS

Some HMS members went along last year (Mendelssohn's ELIJAH) and report that it was well organised.
