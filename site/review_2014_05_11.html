James Ross, Chris Hann & Tom Horn

Spirited Ending to the Haslemere Musical Society's Concert Season

The sold-out concert at Haslemere Hall on 10th May provided an ambitious programme of orchestral and choral music, ranging from the light-hearted, to religious and royal grandeur, to end Haslemere Musical Society's 2013/14 season.

The highlight of the first half was the Scottish Fantasy by Max Bruch, who wrote the piece for the Spanish violin virtuoso of his day and is in reality a complete violin concerto in four movements. This provided no problem for Tom Horn, forsaking his usual role as the orchestra's leader to give full value to the dazzling runs as well as the intimate moments. The first movement proved a somewhat heavy Germanic take on Scottish melody, but this was dispelled by the scherzo second movement, followed by two further movements in which the Scottish melodies became fully apparent. A notable feature was the use of harp, admirably played by Heather Wrighton. The brass section deserves mention here and throughout the concert

The chorus was worked hard by the demanding repertoire, of which the less familiar item was César Franck's triumphal Psalm 150, where they were joined by - and had to compete with - organ and full orchestra. More contemplative pieces were the Bach/Gounod Ave Maria accompanied by harp, Mozart's Ave Verum and Franck's Panis Angelicus; these were sung with quiet sincerity, avoiding the sentimentality sometimes associated with them.

The English Folksong Suite by Surrey composer Ralph Vaughan Williams was given a spirited performance in the second half. Based on three folk tunes, of which the first and last were marches, in which the rhythmic energy was boosted by young percussionists Joe and Sam Horrox, the orchestra, conductor and audience were propelled along merrily towards the finale of the concert.

This was three Coronation Anthems by Handel at his most inspired. The chorus and orchestra were similarly inspired and gave their all to end the evening.

Our thanks to Dr James Ross for his leadership and to Chorus Master Christopher Hann for a fine series of concerts in the season. We look forward to the next concert (with orchestra only, in St Christopher's Church, Weyhill) on Saturday 18th October.

Nicholas Tyrrell-Evans
