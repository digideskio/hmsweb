New Season - Rehearsals

Rehearsals for the new season will start for both choir and orchestra on 9th September, at 7:30pm.

This term the choir will be rehearsing Vivaldi's Gloria for the December concert. The orchestra will be looking at works by Ravel, Fauré and Brahms for the orchestral concert in October. More details about this year's concerts are in the Events section.

New members are always welcome. Our rehearsals for both choir and orchestra take place in Haslemere Hall on Mondays from 7:30pm until about 10pm. For more information see the Join Us section.

News - Chairman's newsletter

As we come to the end of our 2012-2013 season, so it's a good time to reflect on the year that has gone and that which is ahead of us.

In October 2012 we had a wonderful Orchestral concert in St Christopher's church with Mozart's overture to Don Giovanni, the Delius Song before Sunrise and Schubert's Symphony No 6. The highlight of the evening was Helen Owlett's beautiful clarinet playing in Weber's Clarinet Concerto and Finzi's Five Bagatelles.

We are indeed fortunate to have such talent in our Orchestra. The December concert was a sell out for The Messiah (excerpts) and Beethoven's Symphony No 6, the Pastoral. Both were enthusiastically received by the large audience. In February we had a hugely successful Come and Sing in St Christopher's Church when Christopher Hann, our new Chorus Master, rehearsed a choir of over 100 in Faurés Requiem with Clive Osgood on the organ.

We were then joined by a small orchestra (mostly HMS members) for the performance which added much to the pleasure of the event for all present. Christopher has been a great success in everything he has done with us throughout his first season with us.

The performance of Mendelssohn's Elijah in March was outstanding. Orchestra and Chorus were at their best on the night and the soloists were superb. Thanks to the sterling efforts of members of the Society in publicising the concert and selling tickets we had a near full house with only a few unsold seats downstairs.

John Jackson

Welwyn Garden City in March

When the trip to Welwyn Garden City Music Society to perform Elijah on 23rd March with its Orchestra and Chorus was suggested, no-one considered the weather would be so dreadful. On the morning of the trip some members were frightened off by snow and ice. Others were suffering from recurring winter coughs and colds. With the help of a determined driver and comfortable coach 20 members got to St John's Church, Digswell and were given a warm welcome by the WGC Orchestra and Chorus.

Everyone who braved the journey and the long day of rehearsal and performance agreed it was an amazing day: the different venue, joining with another choir and orchestra and the opportunity to sing again a great work which the Society had performed earlier in the month made it a really worthwhile experience.

The photo gives some idea of the weather, the style of the Church and the layout for the concert.

Sue Ecclestone

Highlights of 2011/2012

Membership increased by 1 to 83. We again gave an orchestral concert in St Christopher's Church, Haslemere in October and three orchestral and choral concerts in the Haslemere Hall, in December, March and May, all of which were well received.

For the orchestral concert the church was full and the December concert was near capacity. The October programme consisted of Beethoven's "Coriolan" overture, the Cello Concerto No 1 by Saint-Saens in which Anna Hunt, the leader of our cello section, was the soloist, and Haydn's Symphony No 104.

In December we performed Schumann's Symphony no.3 Rhenish and Mozart's Requiem. In March the programme included Mendelssohn's "Hebrides" Overture, the Symphony no.3 of Sibelius, and Elgar's choral work "The Music Makers".

The May concert, on a Spanish theme, consisted of the Ballet Music from "Le Cid" by Massenet, two items by De Falla, "Danse Espagnole" from "La Vida Breve" and Suite no.1 from "The Three-cornered Hat", and scenes from Bizet's opera "Carmen".

In January the orchestra recorded the music for the soundtrack of a locally-produced film "The Long Journey Home" which was nominated for an award at the Aesthetica Short Film Festival and in May the orchestra played at the Mayor of Haslemere's reception.

We held a successful "Come and Sing" day in February in St Christopher's Church when the work rehearsed and performed was the choral suite from "The Armed Man" by Karl Jenkins.

In June members and visitors enjoyed four open summer sessions, two for orchestra and two for chorus and orchestra, when a variety of music was played through under guest conductors.

During the year we enabled students of the University of Surrey and the University of Creative Arts to make recordings of the orchestra as part of their study courses.

Our new Chorus Master, Christopher Hann joined us for the beginning of the 2013 season.
